Hi! I'm Luke. I'm a scientist who develops computational models of cognition. I currently research and teach at the University of Western Australia.

I am pursuing several streams of basic cognitive research. For example, I examine 'Prospective Memory', the cognitive processes required to perform deferred actions in the future (e.g., to remember to pick up milk on the way home). I also examine inhibitory control, the processes required to voluntarily inhibit or regulate prepotent action (e.g., to stop yourself from scratching an itch).

I regularly translate basic cognitive science into applied science. For example, I have developed computational models to help understand the cognitive control and prospective memory processes underlying conflict detection in air traffic control.

I'm passionate about statistics. I focus on developing and testing comprehensive cognitive process models with Bayesian methods. I typically work in R and Python.
